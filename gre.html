GRE-Graduate Record Examinations

Substitution Method

Substitute variables or specific values into equations to simplify calculations or solve for unknowns. This can often help in handling complex expressions more effectively.

Plugging In Numbers

Test specific numbers or values in place of variables to check the validity of equations or to estimate solutions. This technique is particularly useful for quantitative comparison questions.

Backsolving

Start from the answer choices and work backward to see which one satisfies the given conditions or equations. This method is beneficial for multiple-choice questions.

Estimation and Approximation

Use approximation techniques to quickly estimate solutions without solving complex equations fully. This can save time, especially in the quantitative comparison section.

Using Formulas Efficiently

Memorize and apply important mathematical formulas, especially those related to geometry, algebra, and arithmetic. Being familiar with these formulas can speed up calculations.
